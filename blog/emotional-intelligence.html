Emotional-Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional inteligence is the ability to understand your own feelings and to effectively emphasise with others, to defuse conflict and to communicate clearly. A person with good EQ can recognise, control and express one’s own emotion’s, perceive and assess other’s emotions.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is a measure of a persons academic intelligence that is measured as a number,which is used to express the intelligence of a person. This can be determined by a persons performance on a standardized intelligence test relative to the average performance of others of the same age. EQ (an acronym for the term Emotional Quotient),is a person’s capacity to recognize his/her own and others’ emotions. It is a measurement of an individual’s emotional intelligence, which distinguishes between distinct feelings and uses this intelligence to guide thought and behavior.

Why Is it Important

Emotional intelligence is the ability to identify and control your emotions and understand the emotions the others.When a workforce actively develops emotional intelligence it benefits them personally and professionally. Employees can identify personal triggers that cause emotional distress at work and help maintain a positive attitude,recognize social cues and sense the emotional states of team members and clients. This benefits the employer immeasurably as an emotionally intelligent employee is more productive and can helps foster positive working relationships.
